Book Analysis: Attitude is everything by Jeff Keller

About Author Jeff Keller

Jeff Keller, President of Attitude is Everything, Inc., works with organizations that want to develop achievers and with people who want to reach their full potential. Jeff is a speaker, seminar leader and writer in the area of motivation and human potential. For more than 14 years, he has delivered his uplifting presentations to businesses, organizations, groups and educational institutions.

About Book

The central theme of the entire book is centered on the power of positive thinking and imagination, illustrated through anecdotes such as actor Jim Carrey's practice of visualizing success by signing a postdated check for a substantial sum, which ultimately led to achieving even greater success. The author's own journey serves as another example, transitioning from a career in law to becoming a renowned motivational speaker, guided by the belief that "Attitude is everything."

Additionally, the book features a multitude of other inspiring stories, including that of writer David Baldacci, who maintained unwavering dedication to his craft by adhering to a strict routine of daily writing sessions from 10 pm to 2 am. These narratives collectively emphasize the transformative impact of positive thinking, perseverance, and a proactive attitude in achieving one's goals and realizing one's full potential.

Content is divided into 3 important parts:

  1. Success Begins in the Mind: Where author focuses on positive mindset and importance of imagining ourself as the person we want to be.
  2. Watch Your Words: With positive thinking we tend to say positive words and same is true for viscous circle of negative thoughts.
  3. Heaven Helps Those Who Act: And finally with words of commitment action takes place to ultimately achieve what we want. While taking action never count on overnight success.

Story that inspired me the most is about Sally Jessy Raphael:

  • She couldn’t pay her credit card bills for 26 years. She moved 25 times looking for work.
  • She was fired 18 times.
  • She worked for 26 years before she earned an annual salary of $22,000.
  • She occasionally lived on food stamps and slept in her car.

Despite all of her setbacks, she refused to give up her childhood dream of a career in broadcasting. She was willing to keep failing... and keep failing until she succeeded.
